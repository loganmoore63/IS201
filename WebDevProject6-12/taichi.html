TAI CHI
An Ancient Martial Art

Tai Chi is a Chinese Martial Art that has been practiced by people worldwide for centuries. The practice dates back to the year 1000 in China. The foundations of Tai Chi are related to cosmological concepts such as Yin and Yang and Daoism. A harmony with nature and one's surroundings is a key principle of Tai Chi and provides benefits similar to meditation.


One of the main beliefs of ancient Tai Chi developers is that one's source of life, called "Chi", exists in the belly. Thus, many Tai Chi movements emphasize the abdominal region. It is believed that regular practice of Tai Chi will strengthen one's "Chi", enabling them to enjoy an increased quality of life and health. Click here to learn more about the benefits of Tai Chi.


America's Obesity Epidemic

America has long faced an obesity problem. Much of this is due to low activity rates. When we aren't moving, we aren't burning calories. Those leftover calories become fat. Over time, this leads to obesity. The chart below shows obesity rates compared to physical activity rates by state.


Tai Chi is an effective solution because it gets you up and moving, but not in a strenuous manner. This makes it great for those with past injuries or limited mobility. Even very obese poeple can perform simple Tai Chi movements. This calorie burn will prevent further obesity and stimulate muscle growth.


Benefits of Tai Chi

Here are the benefits of practicing Tai Chi identified by Healthline.com, Harvard Health, and the National Institutes of Health.

  1. Happiness
    • Tai Chi's slow movements and deep breaths can help reduce stress and anxiety
    • It provides the same benefits for managing stress-related anxiety as traditional exercise
    • Tai Chi is very accessible and lower impact than many other forms of exercise
    • The slow, mindful breaths and movements have a positive effect on the nervous system and mood-regulating hormones
  2. Better Sleep
    • Regularly practicing Tai Chi may help you to have more restful sleep
    • It can improve sleep for older adults, too
  3. Weight Loss
    • Regularly practicing Tai Chi can result in weight loss
    • One study tracked changes in weight in a group of adults practicing Tai Chi five times a week for 45 minutes and saw significant results
  4. Balance and Flexibility
    • Tai Chi can help maintain strength, flexibility, and balance
    • The movements are usually circular and never forced, the muscles are relaxed rather than tensed, the joints are not fully extended or bent, and connective tissues are not stretched
  5. Management of Chronic Conditions
    • Tai Chi may help people with fibromyalgia sleep better and cope with pain, fatigue, and depression
    • It can be easily adapted for anyone, from the most fit to people confined to wheelchairs or recovering from surgery
